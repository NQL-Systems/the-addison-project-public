# About the Builder

I’m a self-taught builder working at the intersection of AI systems, documentation, workflow design, and agent orchestration.

My background is not a traditional software engineering path. I’m learning a lot of this in motion while building real systems, testing ideas directly, and tightening my understanding through structured iteration. That matters to me, because I do not want to present borrowed polish as mastery. I would rather be honest about what I know, what I can prove, and what I am still growing into.

A large part of my work involves using frontier models and coding agents as collaborators under bounded direction. I do not treat agents as magic or as replacements for judgment. I use them to help me think, draft, audit, implement, compare approaches, and move faster across areas where I am still building depth. The work is still shaped by human decisions, architectural intent, verification, and correction.

That is part of what this repository represents.

I am interested in how a person without a formal engineering pedigree can still build serious systems by combining discipline, documentation, orchestration, and careful use of AI tools. I care a lot about truthfulness in claims, structure over hype, and learning in a way that leaves behind artifacts other people can inspect.

I am still learning. That is not a branding angle. It is simply true.

What I hope to do in public is make that learning legible while building things that are real enough to examine, question, and improve. If you are someone with deeper technical experience, clearer language for a problem I am reaching toward, or useful criticism about architecture, boundaries, or proof, that kind of feedback is welcome.

This project is not meant to present me as finished.
It is meant to show the shape of the work honestly.
